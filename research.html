Why Neurobiophysics?

On the recent past, there has been a significantly increase on biology researches by the so called exact sciences, such as physics, mathemathics and computer science. As an example, complex algorithm are been developed by researches without background in biology for data processing of the human genome.

This seems to be particularly true in the Neurosciences. Since a few decades, exciting advances have and are been reached, such as identify neuronal sinaptic pathways and receptive fields in an attempt to understante de information flux from the sensory organs to the cortex and back do the motor system. However we still don't have a quantitative knowledge of how this this relate and how they work. Basically, we don't have a cheap robot able to buy our breakfast on the nearest bakery. We only can say that we understand a biological system when we are able to build a similar tool with similar efficiency. Physicists are confident that they can make a great contribution on this effort, isolating a biological subsystem and learn from the biologists what are the variables and parameters which can be controlled.

One of our goals is to analyse the brain and its constituints from the Complex Systems approach and to obtain a structural understading from its dynamics. To monitor the brain activity using technics from the physics provoked a truly revolution, allowing the researcher to almost "read the thoughts" of the subject. Multi-electrode registering made possible to acquire a large amount of data simultaneously, which boosted the development of new mathematical techniques to analyse it.

We are, nonetheless, very far away from a understanding of the brain modus operandi. To approach this herculean task, the most obvious thig to do is investigate simpler systems, mimicking Galileu on his famous free fall studies. Nature tends to recycle solutions that worked in simpler situations. The greates difficulty to use the experimental and theoretical practices oh Physics to biological researches if to make in vivo experiments and analyse them in a context with a lot of parameters to be observed and controlled with a minimum interference on the animal behaviour. Besides, all the results, when on same conditions, shoud be reproducible within permissible errors and unavoidable biological noise.
