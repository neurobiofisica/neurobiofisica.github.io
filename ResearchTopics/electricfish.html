Electrocommunication and neural code

One of the main issues on modern neuroscience is how to decode the so called "neural code". Somehow all of our sensory stimuli are encoded in our neural networks as electric patterns of neural activation. As the information flows through the cells, the synapses and the internal dynamics process this "electric language", generating behaviours, feelings, thoughts; analogous as a processor computes a "digital language" into apps, cellphone translate radio signals into sound, industrial machines (?) actions. To understand what are the transformation rules of this language processing tells us what are the limits of the inherent process coded by this language.

Usually, real-time interaction with neurons involves invasive procedures, such as cirurgical introduction of electrodes (sometime intracellularly), which can interfere on the normal behaviour of the system. Despite this limitations, many results have been achieved with this kind of studies. However, following the Krogh's principle from neuroethology ("for such a large number of problems there will be some animal of choice, or a few such animals, on which it can be most conveniently studied") some researches found the electric fish as good models for developing tools to understand neural code.

Weakly electric fihes have a self-generated electric pattern, very similar to the ondes generated by neurons, which is detected by electroreceptors on their skin. By the distortion of the expected field of a clean environment, they can use it to locate theirselves through the space - called active electrolocation. By the dection of other fish discharge, they can communicate, solving territorial dominance or sexual issues. Their signal can be easily detected by just putting some electrodes in the water, without interfering directly with the fish physiology. On the other hand, a couple of wires can send a electric signal and behave as a "robot fish", allowing a real-time "conversation" with a computer model, for example.

By using Dynamical Systems, Information Theory and Stochastic Problems, we can develop tools to make a real-time interaction which correlates the fish electric signal with its behaviour to create a controlled stimuli, and analyze what are the relevant model parameters for the animal communication, and, by that, determine the "grammar" of this "language". The development of such tools for experimental procedures, data analysis and the correlation of that with neural code are the main issues of our research.
